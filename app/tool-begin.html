Self-Soothing

Just Breathe. One of the most effective ways to activate the relaxation response is by decreasing the heart rate with deep breathing techniques.

Get Organized. We often get anxious or stressed out because we feel like we’ve forgotten to do something. Eliminate that by using a calendar. Being organized will stave off some stress and anxiety because you’re not so flustered.

No Caffeine or Alcohol. Studies have found that the stimulating effects of caffeine can cause anxiety, trigger panic attacks, and increase feelings of nervousness and irritability.

Eat Well-Balanced Meals. Eating the right foods our bodies need will help stave off stress and anxiety. Fruits, veggies, and foods with high omega-3 fatty acids have been shown to reduce stress.

Chew Gum. Chewing gum reduces cortisol levels to ease stress.

Get a Good Night's Sleep. Losing just a few hours of sleep increases feelings of stress, anger, sadness, and exhaustion.

Take a Nap. Naps reduce cortisol levels, which decreases stress.

Get a Massage. A relaxing massage will help ease your body of tension and free your mind of the worries and stresses of the day.

Use a Heating Pad.Heat relieves tension and stress around the neck and shoulders. You can also put the heating pad on your chest when you feel anxious to help relieve that feeling.

Try Aromatherapy. Scents like lavender help reduce stress levels. Light some candles and breathe in the aromatic scents and feels your worries wash away.

Tea Time. Drinking black tea can help lower the stress hormone, cortisol, levels and lead to relaxation.

Music. Listening to music is a great escape from the day-to-day responsibilities we have. Turn on your favorite music and sing along.

Listen to Calming Sounds. Listening to soothing, calming sounds like a thunder storm, waves crashing at the beach, or birds chirping on a spring day, lowers stress and anxiety levels.

Exercise. Exercise releases endorphins to make you happy that snuff out cortisol.

Phone-a-Friend. Chatting with a friend can ease stress and anxiety. It can also give you a new perspective on your current situation.

Get a Touchstone. A touchstone is something small and smooth. You hold it in your hand and rub it gently with your thumb when you’re stressed or anxious.

Distraction

Take a Time-Out. Do a puzzle, read a book, look at artwork, make some crafts. Stepping back from the problem helps clear your head.

Opposite Action

Affirmations and Inspiriation. Looking at inspirational and uplifting quotes or images will help soothe away your worries and fears.

Laugh. Laughing decreases cortisol and adrenaline and tricks your nervous system into making you happy.

Emotional Awareness

Positive Self-Talk. To increase emotional comfort, it's imperative to practice reassuring and realistic self-talk. When anxious, practice self-talk phrases such as:

"This feeling will pass."

"I will get through this."

"I am safe right now."

"I am feeling anxious now, but I have the power make myself calm."

"I can feel my heart rate slowing down."

Write Down Your Worries. Keeping a journal is a great way to express all of your feelings and learn from them. Grab a pen and a notebook and write down all of your stresses and what makes you anxious.

Mindfulness

Focus on the Here and Now. Stress typically strikes when we’re stuck in the past or fretting about the future, Hibbert said. She suggested the following strategies to focus on the present:“At any given moment in the day, simply stop, take a deep breath, and notice where you are, notice what is happening [and] take it all in.Imagine a big brick wall popping up that blocks you from thinking of anything but what’s right in front of you.Get in tune with your senses: Take a walk, feel the ground beneath your feet, smell the flowers in the air, listen to the birds chirping. You will decrease your anxiety and increase your joy by learning to focus on now.”
